Resources You Should AVOID

Based on 12+ years of study and real court testing, these resources lead to failure

Social Media Scams

  • Instagram "Guru" Pages: Selling "equity" and "discharge" eBooks with zero evidence
  • Facebook "Sovereign" Groups: Spreading failed theories and fake success stories
  • TikTok "Legal Hacks": 60-second pseudolegal advice that will get you in trouble
  • YouTube "Jurisdiction" Channels: Clickbait titles promising impossible results
  • Telegram "Secret" Groups: Exclusive access to "insider" legal theories that don't work

Pseudolegal Websites & Scams

  • Sovereign Citizen Forums: Echo chambers spreading failed theories
  • "Redemption" Websites: Birth certificate nonsense that doesn't work
  • "Common Law Court" Sites: Made-up legal systems with no authority
  • UCC Misinterpretation Scams: Commercial law twisted beyond recognition
  • YouTube "No Jurisdiction" Channels: Spreading failed theories for views and eBook sales
  • Social Media "Equity" Scammers: IG, FB, TikTok pages selling pseudolegal eBooks

Failed Theories & False Claims

  • Admiralty Law Claims: Gold fringe flag mythology
  • "Legal Fiction" Arguments: Judges don't know what you're talking about
  • "Special Visitation" Claims: Made-up legal terminology
  • Article I vs III Confusion: Misunderstood court jurisdiction
  • False Success Stories: Anyone claiming they "won" using these theories in real criminal courts
  • "Courts Have No Jurisdiction" Claims: Fundamental misunderstanding of how law works

Why These Resources Fail

After 12+ years of study and testing these theories in real courts since 2023, I can definitively say:

  • • Judges dismiss or ignore these arguments completely
  • • They make you look foolish in court
  • • They distract from real constitutional protections that work
  • • They waste time and money on failed strategies

The Modern eBook Scam Warning

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • • Social media pages promising "debt discharge" or "equity" claims
  • • Anyone selling "secret" legal knowledge for $19.99-$99
  • • Claims they "beat the system" without showing real court documents
  • • YouTube channels with clickbait titles about "courts having no jurisdiction"
  • • Instagram or TikTok "gurus" with luxury cars but no legal credentials

Remember: If these theories worked, why aren't these people practicing law? Why are they selling eBooks instead of representing clients in court?
